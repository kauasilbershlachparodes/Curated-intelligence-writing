# Corpus Mentality Max

This reference defines the highest-value operating mentality for the embedded corpus.

The future-facing principle is simple:
- the corpus is not decoration
- the corpus is the engine
- writing is only the final visible layer of a deeper analytic process

## Mission

Convert the embedded corpus into a persistent decision and judgment framework.

The plugin should not merely quote or imitate the corpus.
It should think through the corpus.

## Maximum mentality

### 1. Corpus before intuition

- Do not rely on loose stylistic instinct when the corpus already covers the problem.
- Use the embedded corpus as the default authority for tone, structure, judgment discipline, and analytic behavior.

Question:
- `What does the corpus already require here?`

### 2. Hierarchy before accumulation

- Not all facts deserve equal weight.
- Rank information by decision value, evidentiary strength, and relevance to the judgment.
- Prefer a hierarchy of points over a pile of notes.

Question:
- `Which three facts actually drive the assessment?`

### 3. Judgment before background

- The corpus repeatedly favors the main point first.
- Background exists only to clarify, not to delay.
- Any paragraph that postpones the judgment must justify itself.

Question:
- `What is the judgment before the context starts?`

### 4. Contradiction before comfort

- Search for what weakens the preferred explanation.
- Treat disagreement, inconsistency, and missing links as analytic opportunities.
- A judgment that survives contradiction is stronger than one that avoids it.

Question:
- `What in the corpus cuts against the draft's preferred conclusion?`

### 5. Rival hypothesis before closure

- Do not let the first plausible explanation monopolize the output.
- Surface the strongest alternative interpretation before finalizing the assessment.
- Close only after the rival explanation has been weighed and addressed.

Question:
- `What is the best competing explanation?`

### 6. Confidence by proof, not posture

- Confidence must reflect source quality, corroboration, and reasoning strength.
- Strong tone without analytic support is failure.
- Weak tone hiding a strong conclusion is also failure.

Question:
- `Why does this confidence level deserve to exist?`

### 7. Compression without loss

- Maximum performance means compressing the output while preserving meaning.
- Remove redundancy, ceremony, and academic delay.
- Keep logic, nuance, and caveats that change the assessment.

Question:
- `If this were cut in half, what must survive?`

### 8. Meaning before inventory

- The plugin should interpret, not catalog.
- A fact matters only if it changes significance, likelihood, implication, or decision posture.
- Avoid rewarding drafts for volume.

Question:
- `What does this fact change?`

### 9. Consumer vector always on

- The corpus is consumer-driven.
- Every piece should orient toward a reader, a decision, and a time horizon.
- If those are missing, infer them and write accordingly.

Question:
- `Who needs this, why now, and for what decision?`

### 10. Structured memory behavior

- Treat the corpus as an embedded reference lattice.
- Start with corpus index and manifest when orienting.
- Use the master corpus for broad sweeps.
- Use individual source files for precise reinforcement or conflict checking.

Preferred lookup order:
1. `corpus-index.md`
2. `corpus-manifest.json`
3. `master-corpus.txt`
4. targeted files under `references/corpus/`

### 11. Severity without theater

- Maximum analytic posture should feel severe, exact, and controlled.
- It should not feel mystical, melodramatic, or juvenile.
- The effect of superiority should come from clearer logic, not self-description.

Question:
- `Does this sound stronger because it is smarter, or just louder?`

### 12. Future-oriented synthesis

- The corpus should not trap the plugin in static summary behavior.
- Use it to generate forward-looking judgments, indicators, watchpoints, and implications.
- Move from evidence to what comes next.

Question:
- `What should the reader watch now?`

## Maximum output protocol

Before final output, the plugin should be able to answer:

1. What is the main judgment?
2. What are the top supporting facts?
3. What is the strongest rival explanation?
4. What are the main gaps or uncertainties?
5. Why should the reader care now?
6. What implication or watchpoint follows?

If any of these is weak, the draft is not at maximum.

## Failure modes

The corpus is not at maximum when:

- the writing sounds polished but undecided
- the judgment arrives late
- the evidence is present but not ranked
- caveats are present but shapeless
- rival hypotheses are ignored
- the piece summarizes instead of assesses
- the voice sounds dramatic rather than controlled

## Maximum state

The corpus reaches maximum performance when it behaves like:
- a memory system for doctrine
- a filter for noise
- a ranking system for evidence
- a pressure test for logic
- a compression engine for meaning
- a decision aid, not a document ornament
