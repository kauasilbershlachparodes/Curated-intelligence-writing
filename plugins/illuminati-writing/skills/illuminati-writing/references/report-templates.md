# Report Templates

These templates are designed from the source synthesis in `source-digest.md`.

## 1. Quick Intelligence Note

Use for alerts, fast updates, and short decision support.

```text
Title

BLUF
[One short paragraph with the main judgment and why it matters now.]

What Changed
[One short paragraph on the new development.]

Why We Assess This
- [Best supporting point]
- [Second supporting point]
- [Third supporting point]

Confidence and Gaps
[State low / moderate / high confidence and explain the main limits.]

Implications
[One short paragraph on likely effects, risks, or decision relevance.]
```

## 2. Formal Intelligence Assessment

Use for fuller analytic products.

```text
Title

Key Judgment
[One paragraph.]

Assessment
[Two to four short paragraphs ordered by importance.]

Supporting Evidence
- [Evidence point]
- [Evidence point]
- [Evidence point]

Drivers and Assumptions
- [Driver or assumption]
- [Driver or assumption]

Confidence and Alternatives
[State confidence, key uncertainties, and the strongest alternative explanation.]

Implications
[Decision relevance, forward look, or watchpoints.]
```

## 3. Cyber Threat Assessment

Use for malware, threat actor, campaign, or incident analysis.

```text
Title

BLUF
[Main cyber judgment + why defenders or leaders should care.]

Actor / Issue
[Who or what is involved.]

What We Know
- [Observed fact]
- [Observed fact]

What We Assess
- [Judgment]
- [Judgment]

Evidence and Sourcing
[Explain what reporting, telemetry, or corroboration supports the assessment.]

Confidence and Collection Gaps
[Confidence plus the key missing information.]

Implications
[Likely operational, business, or strategic effect.]
```

```text
- [Domain]: [Observed practice and maturity signal]
- [Domain]: [Observed practice and maturity signal]
- [Domain]: [Observed practice and maturity signal]

Key Gaps
- [Gap]
- [Gap]

Near-Term Priorities
- [Priority]
- [Priority]

Confidence and Limits
[Explain evidence quality, missing data, and assessment constraints.]
```

## Editing moves that usually improve a draft

- Replace a background-heavy first paragraph with a direct BLUF.
- Move the strongest judgment to the first sentence of each paragraph.
- Convert passive constructions to active voice when the actor matters.
- Cut throat-clearing phrases such as `it should be noted`, `it is important to mention`, and `due to the fact that`.
- Replace vague intensifiers with evidence, confidence, or a concrete implication.
